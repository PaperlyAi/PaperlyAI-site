Paperly AI — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 29 May 2026  ·  Effective: 29 May 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what personal data the Paperly AI mobile application (com.everydaycreative.paperly, the “App”) processes, why, who it is shared with, and the rights you have under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and equivalent laws. It is written to be read in a few minutes by a non-lawyer.

The short version

  • Your scanned documents are stored only on your phone. We run no account system and keep no copy of your documents on our servers.
  • To read the text from a scan, the App sends a copy to Google’s Gemini AI over an encrypted connection, through a small proxy we operate. This is the core function of the App.
  • On the paid Google service we use, Google does not use your documents to train its AI. Google keeps a copy for up to 55 days only to detect abuse, then deletes it automatically.
  • No ads, no tracking, no analytics profiles, no selling of data. The only identifier we send is a random per-install token used to prevent abuse.
  • You stay in control: delete any document in the App, or uninstall to remove everything on your device.

3. Where your data goes (recipients & processors)

3.1 On your device

Every document you scan is saved in the App’s private storage on your device, along with the extracted fields. This is the primary and permanent home of your data. We operate no cloud library of your documents.

3.2 Our processing proxy — Cloudflare

When you scan, the App sends a downscaled copy of the image (or your PDF) over an encrypted HTTPS connection to a small proxy we run on Cloudflare Workers. The proxy checks the anonymous token, applies abuse limits, and forwards the document to Google (Section 3.3). The proxy does not store your documents; it retains only the anonymous token and aggregate request counters needed for abuse prevention. Cloudflare acts as our processor under its Data Processing Addendum and relies on Standard Contractual Clauses for any transfers outside the EEA.

3.3 Text extraction — Google Gemini API (paid tier)

The proxy sends your document to Google’s Gemini API (generativelanguage.googleapis.com) to extract the text and fields. We use the paid tier of this service, which is governed by Google’s Gemini API Additional Terms of Service and the Data Processing Addendum for Products Where Google is a Data Processor. Under these terms, for paid use:

  • No AI training. Google does not use your prompts, the uploaded document, or the generated output to train or improve its AI models.
  • Limited retention. Google logs the request for up to 55 days solely to detect abuse and enforce its policies, then deletes it automatically. This abuse-monitoring data is not used to train general AI models.
  • Limited human access. A person at Google may review content only if it is flagged by automated safety/abuse systems, or where required by law.
  • Google acts as a data processor on our behalf for these documents. Its sub-processors are listed at business.safety.google/subprocessors/.

3.4 Optional AI features — Anthropic

If — and only if — you enable the optional summary/chat features and enter your own Anthropic API key, the App sends the relevant document text directly from your device to api.anthropic.com. These requests do not pass through our proxy, and your key never reaches us. That processing is governed by Anthropic’s privacy terms. This feature is off by default.

4. International data transfers

Our processors (Google, Cloudflare) may process data on servers outside the European Economic Area, including in the United States. Where this happens, the transfer is protected by the European Commission’s Standard Contractual Clauses and, for Google, by Google’s certification under the EU–US Data Privacy Framework, together with the technical and organisational safeguards described in their respective Data Processing Addenda.

6. Automated decision-making

The App uses AI to read and structure your documents, but it does not make any automated decision that produces legal or similarly significant effects about you (GDPR Art. 22). The extracted results are shown to you to use as you see fit.

7. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we process about you;
  • Rectify inaccurate data;
  • Erase your data (“right to be forgotten”);
  • Restrict or object to processing;
  • Data portability; and
  • Withdraw consent at any time, where processing is based on consent.

Because your documents live on your device, you can exercise most of these rights yourself at any time: delete any document in the App, or uninstall the App to erase everything stored on your device. The copy held by Google for abuse monitoring expires automatically within 55 days. For anything else — including a formal access or erasure request directed to our processors — contact us at paperlyai@proton.me and we will respond within one month.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority, in particular in your country of residence. For users in Greece, the competent authority is the Hellenic Data Protection Authority (HDPA), www.dpa.gr.

8. Children

The App is not directed to children under 16, and we do not knowingly process children’s data. If you believe a child has provided personal data through the App, contact us and we will delete it.

9. Security

Data in transit is protected with TLS/HTTPS encryption. Documents stored on your device are kept in the App’s private, OS-protected storage, and any API key you enter is held in the platform secure keystore (iOS Keychain / Android Keystore). Our processors maintain recognised security standards (for example, Google maintains ISO 27001 certification and encrypts stored data).
